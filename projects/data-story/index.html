In modern society, people are paying more and more attention to health problems, regarding sleep (50%) and exercise (40%) as two of the three main issues that have great impact on their overall health and welbeing.

In this sense, starting from National Sleep Foundation's Sleep in America Poll , this project aims at revealing a more direct relationship between sleep and exercise via data visualization.

ABOUT SLEEP

the lighter, the younger

‚óè The elderly tend go to bed early and have shorter sleep duration compared with younger people.


the bigger, the worse

‚óè The majority goes to bed at around 11 p.m, with an average sleep duration of 7~8 hours.

‚óè Shorter sleep duration often leads to worse sleep condition; Same for going to bed quite early in the morning.

‚óè People sleeping over 12 hours seem to be the group with best sleep quality in general.

‚óè It's quite clear that vigorous exercisers always have the smallest percentage of suffering the above sleeping problems.

‚óè Moderate exercisers and light exercisers perform about the same level in terms of these sleeping problems, but non-exercisers without doubt suffer most.

‚óè In this sense, "more exercise, better sleep" seems to be a convincing conclusion according to this poll.
